Climate Change - What is actually happening?

Some of the effects of climate change that are noticeable within our lives today include: glaciers melting at a faster rate than ever before, tropical storms have become more severe, droughts are becoming longer and more extreme and that is just to name a few. So, what is causing this? Many natural and human-induced factors contribute to climate change. Greenhouse gasses form a thick cloud around the earth which absorb and reflect radiation. This means that the radiation is not able to escape from the earth's atmosphere resulting in an increase in heat from the radiation and therefore, progressively warmer temperatures. Increases in human activity which release these greenhouse gasses is why this effect is becoming more severe.
